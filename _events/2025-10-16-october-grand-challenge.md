Understanding social interaction requires the ability to sense and map behaviour
in ways that are unobtrusive, while also capturing the multimodal nature of
social signals and context. In this session, we welcome **Emteq Labs**, who have
been developing a facial wearable sensing device designed to track behaviour
through facial optomyography, while also integrating contextual data via a scene
camera. Such approaches open exciting opportunities for both research and
application. The focus on facial wearables is especially timely, as major players
in human–computer interaction (e.g. Meta, Apple) are investing heavily in
wearable computing platforms with capabilities for capturing and simulating
social behaviour, and for augmenting human–machine and human–human interactions
in VR and AR.

## Abstract

The boundary between the brain’s inner world — its thoughts, emotions, and
cognitive processes — and the external world of perception, interaction, and
behaviour is a critical frontier for both psychology and computer science. Emteq
Labs provides a novel interface at this boundary, offering a real-time, wearable
system that captures affective and cognitive responses through facial muscle
activity, head movement, and other physiological signals. By integrating these
signals with contextual information from an onboard camera, OCOsense enables a
dynamic mapping of internal states to external experiences. This allows for
fine-grained analysis of how users emotionally and cognitively engage with their
surroundings, offering valuable insights into attention, stress, mood, and
behavioural patterns. The ability to synchronise real-time affective sensing with
environmental stimuli opens up significant applications across psychological and
neuroscientific research, product testing, human–computer/robot interaction,
behaviour monitoring, and therapy.

The interactive discussion covered multimodal data integration; technical and
ethical challenges; data, analytics, and interpretation; and future directions.

## Speakers

**Dr Charles Nduka** is Chief Science Officer and founder of Emteq. He studied
medicine at Oxford and Imperial College and is an NHS Consultant Plastic and
Reconstructive Surgeon in London and Sussex. His research focuses on facial
expressions, emotion measurement, mental health, and rehabilitation, with awards
from the Wellcome Trust, Innovate UK, NIHR, and Horizon 2020. Passionate about
health technology, he invented facial optomyography in 2017 for low-power,
all-day facial sensing and biofeedback, and founded Brighton-based Emteq Labs to
scale this technology.

**Professor Hristijan Gjoreski** is the Chief Technology Officer and Chief Data
Scientist at Emteq Labs, and Associate Professor at the Ss. Cyril and Methodius
University of Skopje. He is a recognised expert in applied artificial intelligence
and machine learning, specialising in algorithms for e-health, wearable computing,
activity recognition, and affective computing — enabling new ways to study,
understand, and support human behaviour.
