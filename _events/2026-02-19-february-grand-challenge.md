What does it take to make a digital human talk back to you in real time? This
session examines that question through three distinct lenses: game-engine rigs
like MetaHuman Creator, AI video generation, and Gaussian splatting — each
representing a different philosophy for achieving responsive digital embodiment.

No single approach dominates every dimension. Some prioritize control, others
realism, others speed. What’s most compelling is how these paradigms are beginning
to borrow from one another — and how quickly the boundaries between them are
dissolving.

Invited guest **Yuntian Chai** delivers a clear walkthrough of how each approach
works, live demonstrations, and a thoughtful comparison of their strengths,
trade-offs, and future trajectories. Hosted by **Chen Zhou**.

## Talk — Real-Time Digital Avatars — A Comparative Analysis (Yuntian Chai)

A comparative analysis of real-time digital avatar pipelines: MetaHuman-style
game-engine rigs, diffusion-based AI video generation, and Gaussian splatting.
The talk covers how each works, live demos, and a comparison of control, realism,
latency, and emerging hybrid approaches.

## Speakers

**Yuntian Chai** is a multidisciplinary builder working at the intersection of
art, computer science, and psychology. He is the co-founder of Cogix and has
served as a founding engineer across multiple startups, leading the development
of early-stage products from concept to launch.

Earlier in his career, he worked as a developer and technical engineer within the
Unreal Engine ecosystem at Epic Games, contributing to developer-facing
technologies. He later joined Hedra, where he prototyped the company’s real-time
avatar system powered by diffusion-based video generation.

Yuntian holds degrees spanning psychology, computer science, and art, including
undergraduate and master’s training in Psychology from East China Normal
University, a Master’s degree in Computer Science from the University of
Pennsylvania, and a Master’s degree in Interactive Media from Sheridan College.
He now focuses on building AI-native creative tools that expand the boundaries of
human creation and expression.

## Host

**Dr Chen Zhou** is a Lecturer in the School of Psychology & Neuroscience at the
University of Glasgow. His research combines classic psychophysical methods,
cutting-edge virtual reality technologies, and computational modelling to study
human perception and action in social contexts. He is particularly interested in
modelling human social behaviour and translating these findings into algorithms
that can be implemented in intelligent virtual agents and social robot platforms.
Dr Zhou has extensive experience developing virtual reality programs using Unreal
Engine and conducting behavioural experiments with robotic systems.
