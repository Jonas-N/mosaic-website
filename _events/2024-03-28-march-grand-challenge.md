This session discussed experimental semiotics as a methodological framework for
studying interaction and communication in controlled lab settings, with
presentations from two long-time MOSAIC members and commentary from two invited
discussants.

## Experimental Semiotics: Studying the building blocks of communication in the lab (Jonas Nölle)

Experimental Semiotics (ES) investigates how novel semiotic systems emerge in
interactive lab games. By observing people ground communication from scratch, it
exposes the fundamental principles of semiosis. Based on a recent review, I present
the main experimental paradigms commonly used to identify the pressures and
mechanisms shaping the structure of communication systems, and discuss key findings
from the last 20 years — including some of the building blocks of language, and
cultural and cognitive factors. I close with future directions for ES, including
VR paradigms and neural approaches to studying the social, cognitive, and
biological components of human communication.

## Devil is in the dynamics: continuous co-construction of shared conceptual space (Rui Liu)

During communication, we continuously infer and adjust to each other's changing
mental states. Time is an essential dimension in which such interpersonal exchange
unfolds. I emphasise the theoretical and empirical relevance of studying
communication dynamics, and show two examples of how experimental semiotic games
provide a trackable means of unpacking the temporal dynamics of communication.
