This session opened a discussion about the promises of studying interactions
between more than two individuals, and the unique challenges associated with these
studies. The speakers gave examples from their own research on hearing impairment
and acoustic noise, with the main focus on the contrasts between group
interactions and dyadic interaction. After the presentations, all attendees were
invited to contribute their own stories, frustrations, and insights about how to
tackle the challenges of group interaction research.
