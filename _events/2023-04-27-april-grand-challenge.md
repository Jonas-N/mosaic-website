Our invited guest was **Prof. Guy Itzchakov** (University of Haifa), whose research
focuses on the effects of high-quality listening on speakers, listeners, and their
relationships, including listening-training studies in organisations. Guy first
presented his work on defining high-quality listening and its effects, followed by
a discussion of the obstacles in listening research and the research prospects
opened up by technological advances, such as using avatars as listeners, chatbots,
and other AI tools.

## Abstract

A frequent misperception is that speakers set the tone of conversations, while the
listener is a passive actor — leading people to believe that listening is the same
as being silent, or merely smiling and occasionally nodding. Nevertheless, the
power of listeners to shape the conversation and affect speakers' emotions,
attitudes, and behaviours is enormous. Speakers who converse with good listeners
have better memory of the content they shared, speak more fluently, feel less
anxious, report less prejudiced attitudes, and perform better at work, among many
other effects. The advancement of technology also raises exciting, previously
unexplored questions: can avatars elicit the perception of good listening? Can we
use technology to better understand which listening behaviours matter most? And
how many speaker–listener iterations does it take to elicit listening-induced
outcomes?
