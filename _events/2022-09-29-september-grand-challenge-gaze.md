The meeting was a discussion that built on the following review:

> Hessels, R. S. (2020). How does gaze to faces support face-to-face interaction?
> A review and perspective. *Psychonomic Bulletin & Review, 27*(5), 856–881.

The paper covers functional constraints of gaze for acquiring information from
faces; facial identity, emotional expressions, and gaze; speech perception and
gaze; perception of looking direction; face scanning; observational and
eye-tracking studies of gaze in interaction; and a dynamic-systems perspective on
gaze in face-to-face interaction.

We were delighted that the author, **Dr Roy Hessels** (Utrecht University), joined
us for the discussion, along with two expert guests on gaze behaviour and social
interaction: **Professor Alan Kingstone** (University of British Columbia) and
**Professor Daniel Richardson** (University College London).
