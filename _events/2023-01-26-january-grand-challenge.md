This session showed how social interaction can be studied by having pairs of
participants interact directly in task-oriented laboratory settings.

What makes an interaction go well? We discussed different theoretical models and
output measures that best capture when people interact successfully in
joint problem-solving contexts — such as linguistic alignment and synergy — and
their implications for coordination. And what happens when things don't go so
well? We looked at cases of miscommunication and the self- and other-repair
mechanisms involved in overcoming them. The presentations and discussion also
touched on aspects such as diversity and procedural coordination that have often
been neglected in studies of dyadic interaction and dialogue.

Jonas Nölle introduced the topic, followed by guests **Kristian Tylén** (Aarhus
University / Interacting Minds Centre) and **Gregory Mills** (University of
Groningen).
