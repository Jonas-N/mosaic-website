An inspiring session on building a motion-capture lab, contrasting a high-end
facility with a low-cost portable alternative.

## Motion Capture Lab at the MPI for Psycholinguistics (Sachit Misra)

The Motion Capture Lab at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics is a
cutting-edge facility designed to capture the subtlety of human movement with
precision. It enables researchers to capture motion at very high frame rates and
map relations between language, cognition, and embodied motion through
high-resolution tracking. Equipped with state-of-the-art Vicon cameras, face
tracking, and sync technology, it offers a comprehensive setup for capturing
full-body movement, facial expressions, and micro-movements that traditional video
methods might miss — with powerful tools to recreate motion and build stimuli.

## Donders Mobile Multimodal Lab (Wim Pouw)

While multimodality is increasingly recognised as a natural mode of communication,
empirical investigations into the "hyper" multimodal nature of communication have
been limited by the challenges of setting up, collecting, and analysing
time-varying signals with different modality characteristics. To address this, we
developed a practical manual and coding library for capturing human behaviour in an
ecologically valid, multidimensional framework, using open-source resources to help
researchers assemble a low-cost (~2K–15K), portable lab. It records multiple
frame-synced 2D videos for reconstructed 3D body motion (e.g. with freemocap,
pose2sim), audio, and physiological signals (e.g. ECG, EMG), integrated through the
Lab Streaming Layer for precise synchronisation — making the study of multimodality
easier and more equitable for labs with and without extensive resources.
