This event explored new perspectives on communicative efficiency by adding the
visual modality into the picture and drawing insights from sign languages and
multimodal communication. It featured **International Sign (IS) interpreting**.

## Abstract

Communicative efficiency — the idea that speakers and listeners try to minimise the
costs of communication while maximising the benefits (mutual understanding) — has
been argued to shape the structure of languages. It is typically studied based on
written representations of speech, overlooking the potential complementary
contributions of other channels in face-to-face settings. The visual modality is a
fundamental part of communication, either as the sole modality for linguistic
expression (as in sign languages) or as a resource used alongside speech.

Anita Slonimska discusses recent studies on how affordances and constraints of the
visual modality shape communicative efficiency in sign languages, revealing shared
and distinct solutions for how linguistic systems optimise for efficiency.
Gabriella Vigliocco then shifts from a unimodal to a multimodal perspective,
discussing findings from the few studies of multimodal communicative efficiency and
their implications. Finally, Natalia Levshina contextualises these directions
within current theory before opening the floor for discussion.
