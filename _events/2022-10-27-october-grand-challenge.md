It has been remarkably difficult for social interaction research to study
interactive contexts. Studies analysing "free conversation" recordings can
identify features that covary with behaviour, but only provide correlational
findings. Experiments that control individual factors — using, for example,
scripted interactions or virtual avatars — can support causal inference, but are
limited in ecological validity.

To overcome these limitations, we have been building a new experimental platform
called **DuckSoup**. DuckSoup is a videoconferencing platform, like Zoom or Skype,
but one where researchers can manipulate participants' facial and vocal attributes
in real time using voice and face transformation algorithms — for example
artificially increasing one participant's smiles, or changing the emotional tone
of their voice. This gives researchers the ability to covertly control social
signals during interactions. The session brainstormed what would be possible with
this platform, at an early stage of its testing and development.
