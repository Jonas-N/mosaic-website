This month's meeting was a methods discussion on virtual reality as a tool for
experimental research. Jonas gave an introduction to VR, showcasing
state-of-the-art equipment and software and giving an overview of how it is used
across scientific disciplines — from spatial cognition to education and clinical
applications.

The talk then focused on how VR can be applied specifically in multimodal social
interaction research, drawing on Jonas's own work and experience, and offered a
primer on how to set up your own VR experiment, including best practices and how
to avoid common pitfalls.
