This month's meeting was a discussion and brainstorm surrounding the challenges
of capturing and analysing natural language. The goal was to identify pain points
that prevent us from more fully incorporating this channel of communication into
our research, and potential solutions that members of the group have or would like
to learn. We covered practical challenges (e.g. capturing language in interaction
and over time), conceptual challenges (e.g. what format, modality, or constraints
are best for a given project), and methodological challenges (e.g. conducting
time-dynamic and dyadic analyses).

## Format

Discussion and brainstorm, using the ideas generated as a springboard for future,
more targeted sessions such as methods workshops.

## Agenda

- **Introduction (5 min)** — defining what we mean by "natural language"; overview of the types of challenge.
- **Brainstorm challenges (breakout rooms, 20 min)** — theoretical, conceptual, practical, and methodological.
- **Collate the list (20 min)** — organised by type of challenge to find where consensus lies, and prioritise those most relevant to the group.
- **Discuss potential solutions (20 min)** — chairs present approaches they are familiar with; others invited to share their expertise.
- **Next steps (20 min)** — activities to address these challenges (e.g. methods workshops), and relevant funding opportunities.
- **Wrap-up (5 min).**
