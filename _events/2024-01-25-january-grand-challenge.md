This meeting echoed one of the very first MOSAIC sessions. **Pablo Arias Sarah**
(Marie Curie Fellow, School of Psychology and Neuroscience) presented an update on
DuckSoup, a platform to study and experimentally control social interactions in
real time online. What seemed like science fiction two years earlier had become a
reality, and Pablo gave a demo and led a discussion on the possibilities this new
platform opens for multimodal social interaction data collection and research.

## Abstract

In 2022, at a MOSAIC Grand Challenge, I presented why it is of interest to use
voice/face transformation algorithms to transform participants' voice and face in
real time during free social interactions — giving researchers the ability to make
causal inferences between transformed attributes (e.g. vocal gender, smile
intensity) and subsequent behaviours (e.g. social outcomes, expressive synchrony).

This year I introduce the experimental platform DuckSoup, a free and open-source
platform we developed to this end. I provide a demo of how to use DuckSoup
alongside other free and open-source technologies to run, collect, and analyse
social interaction experiments online: how to design an interactive experiment
using the Python package oTree, how to deploy it online, how to run it with real
participants, and finally how to analyse video recordings to extract vocal, facial,
and semantic information. We close with a discussion of the benefits of scaling
social interaction research, the possibilities opened by novel video-analysis
pipelines, and how these could help address key theoretical questions.
