This was the final meeting of the academic year. **Dr Judith Holler** — Research
Group Leader in the Department of Communication in Social Interaction at the MPI in
Nijmegen and Associate Professor at the Donders Institute for Brain, Cognition &
Behaviour, Radboud University — presented her research on the integration of
visual signals with speech.

## Abstract

I focus on the contribution of different visual signals (hand gestures and facial
signals in particular) to utterance formation, semantically and pragmatically —
in terms of their form and timing, as well as their influence on comprehension in
conversational settings. I argue that visual signalling is not only core to human
language in providing additional information, but that its presence facilitates
fast comprehension and responding in interaction, and dyadic coordination more
generally.
