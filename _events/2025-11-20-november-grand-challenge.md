ELAN is a free multimedia annotation tool developed at the Max Planck Institute
for Psycholinguistics. Over the past 25 years, it has become a cornerstone in
research on language and communication — especially in fields that require
detailed, time-aligned annotation of audiovisual data, such as gesture studies,
sign language research, and conversation analysis, as well as research on
interaction in both naturalistic and experimental settings.

Today, ELAN is expanding beyond manual annotation. With recent advances in
automatic speech-to-text transcription, motion-capture technology, and
computer-vision tools, ELAN is increasingly integrated into workflows that
combine human expertise with automated processing. This opens up new
possibilities for more robust, faster, and richer analyses of multimodal
interaction. In this session, we look at both traditional approaches to working
with ELAN and emerging opportunities to combine it with current technological
developments.
