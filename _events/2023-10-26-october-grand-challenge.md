Communicative movements such as gestures are often studied categorically — but they
don't have to be. In this hands-on tutorial, Wim Pouw went over research and
programming routines to study gesture as an ensemble of relational events.
Specifically, he covered the use of bivariate time-series analyses to construct
kinematic spaces and gesture networks (with an accompanying Python tutorial).
Building on previous work — for example on silent gestures — this method has proven
useful for tracking systematicity in evolving silent-gesture systems and detecting
the emergence of kinematic dialects. He also showed how these results can be
integrated into dynamic data dashboards, and introduced the
[envisionbox.org](https://www.envisionbox.org) initiative, a platform for
showcasing multimodal signal-processing routines aimed at making methods in the
field more transparent and fostering cumulative science.

Participation had no prerequisites; those wishing to follow the hands-on portion
were asked to install the software beforehand.
